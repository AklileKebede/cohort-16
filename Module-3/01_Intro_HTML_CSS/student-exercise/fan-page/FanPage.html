Short History Summary

Linkin Park is an American rock band from Agoura Hills, California. Formed in 1996, Linkin Park rose to international fame with their debut studio album, Hybrid Theory (2000). During the years the band's lineup changed, see below members timeline:

Name Activity Years Role
Current members
Mike Shinoda 1996–present vocals, rapping, rhythm guitar, keyboard, samples, synthesizer
Brad Delson 1996–present lead guitar, backing vocals
Dave Farrell 1996–present bass, backing vocals
Joe Hahn 1996–present turntables, samples, programming, backing vocals
Rob Bourdon 1996–present drums, percussion, occasional backing vocals
Former members
Chester Bennington 1999–2017; died 2017 lead vocals, occasional rhythm guitar
Mark Wakefield 1996–1998 lead vocals

Chester Bennington Legacy

    Jonathan McAloon of The Daily Telegraph commented,"Bennington’s death will have an impact on many millennials because his voice was the sound of their millennium".
    Writing for The Guardian, Ben Beaumont-Thomas noted "Bennington’s decision to sing clearly and openly was, therefore, more radical than he is given credit for, and indeed more socially valuable". The journalist continued to discuss Bennington's impact, commenting, His cleanly articulated tales of emotional struggle gave millions the sense that someone understood them, and the huge sound of his band around him magnified that sense, moving listeners from the psychic space of their bedrooms into an arena of thousands of people who shared their pain."
